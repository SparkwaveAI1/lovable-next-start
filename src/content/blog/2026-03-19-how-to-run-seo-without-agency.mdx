# How to Run SEO Without an Agency (And Actually Get Results)

SEO agencies charge $2,000–$10,000/month. For many SMBs, that's not a realistic budget — and even when it is, the results often don't justify the cost.

Here's what most agencies won't tell you: the core of SEO is systematic execution, not rocket science. You don't need an agency. You need a process.

This post breaks down how to run effective SEO with a lean in-house team.

---

## Why Most In-House SEO Efforts Fail

The problem usually isn't knowledge or intent. It's one of three things:

**1. No prioritization system.** Audits generate dozens of issues. Without a clear framework for what to fix first, teams either try to do everything (and finish nothing) or fix the easy stuff (that doesn't move rankings).

**2. No consistent content production.** SEO requires publishing content that targets real keywords people are searching. Most businesses publish sporadically or write content based on gut feeling rather than keyword data.

**3. No measurement loop.** They don't track what's working, so they keep doing things that don't move rankings and stop doing things that do.

Fix these three things and you don't need an agency.

## The In-House SEO System

### Step 1: Get the Baseline Right (Month 1)

Before you can grow organic traffic, your technical foundation needs to be solid.

Run a full technical audit and address the high-priority issues first (see our post on [what actually matters in an SEO audit](link)):
- Fix indexation issues
- Improve Core Web Vitals
- Fix internal linking gaps
- Clean up duplicate content

This isn't glamorous work. It's also the work that agencies charge $2,000/month to do once, and then bill you for monthly retainer while you wonder what they're actually doing.

Most technical foundations can be cleaned up in 2–4 weeks with a developer and a prioritized issue list.

### Step 2: Build Your Keyword Map

A keyword map is a document that assigns target keywords to each page on your site. It ensures:
- Every important page is targeting a specific search term
- You're not competing with yourself (multiple pages targeting the same keyword)
- You have a clear content roadmap for what pages to create next

How to build it:
1. List all your existing pages
2. Use keyword research to identify the best target keyword for each page
3. Identify gaps — topics your competitors rank for that you have no content on
4. Create a list of new pages to build based on those gaps

This takes a few hours. Update it quarterly.

### Step 3: Create Content Consistently

Content is how you acquire new keywords and grow organic traffic over time.

A realistic content cadence for a lean team:
- 2–4 blog posts per month targeting keyword opportunities
- Quarterly updates to your highest-traffic existing pages
- Regular content on your money pages (homepage, service pages) as the business evolves

The content doesn't need to be long. It needs to match what someone is actually searching for. A 600-word post that precisely answers a specific question will outrank a 2,000-word post that meanders.

Use keyword research data to validate your topics before you write. Every piece of content should target a specific keyword with clear search intent.

### Step 4: Build Links Strategically

Backlinks are still a major ranking factor. You don't need hundreds of them. You need relevant, quality links from real websites.

Tactics that work for SMBs:
- **Partner and supplier links** — companies you work with should link to you from their website
- **Local citations** — get listed in relevant directories and industry sites
- **Guest posts** — write for publications your target customers read
- **PR and news coverage** — earned media from your local business community
- **Resource link building** — create genuinely useful content that others reference

Avoid link buying and low-quality link services. One good link from a real industry publication is worth 50 links from generic directories.

### Step 5: Measure What Matters

Three numbers worth tracking monthly:
1. **Organic traffic** — are more people finding you via search?
2. **Keyword rankings** — are your target keywords moving up?
3. **Conversions from organic** — is search traffic actually turning into leads or customers?

Set up Google Search Console (free) and Google Analytics. Review the data monthly. Ask: what worked? What didn't? Adjust the next month's priorities accordingly.

## The Tools You Actually Need

You don't need five tools. You need:

1. **A technical audit and keyword research tool** — Sparkwave AI, Ahrefs, or Semrush
2. **Google Search Console** — free, non-negotiable
3. **Google Analytics** — free, tracks your traffic and conversions

That's it. The rest is execution.

---

## What an Agency Actually Gives You

Agencies are worth the money when:
- You don't have anyone in-house with time or inclination to work on SEO
- You're in a highly competitive vertical and need experienced strategic direction
- You're at significant scale and need ongoing expertise, not just execution

For most SMBs, especially early-stage businesses, what an agency provides is:
1. A technical audit (which you can run yourself)
2. A keyword strategy (which you can build yourself with keyword research tools)
3. Content production (which you can do in-house or outsource directly)
4. Monthly reporting (which you can get from Google Search Console)

When you break it down, most of what agencies charge $3,000/month for is process and tooling — not specialized expertise.
