# Synthetic Research vs. Traditional Research: An Honest Cost and Speed Comparison

If you're evaluating synthetic research for your team, you probably have two questions: How much does it cost compared to traditional methods? And how fast is it?

Fair questions. Here are honest answers.

This is not a pitch for why synthetic is better. Each method has situations where it outperforms the other. The goal is to give you enough information to decide which approach fits your specific research need — or whether you should use both.

## Speed Comparison

### Traditional Qualitative Research

A standard qualitative study — 10-15 in-depth interviews or 2-3 focus groups — follows this timeline:

| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Define research questions and screener | 2-5 days |
| Recruit participants | 2-4 weeks |
| Schedule and conduct sessions | 1-2 weeks |
| Transcription and coding | 3-5 days |
| Analysis and report | 1-2 weeks |
| **Total** | **4-8 weeks** |

The recruiting phase is the bottleneck. Finding 12 qualified participants who match your criteria, are available during study hours, and actually show up takes longer than any other step. For niche B2B audiences (specific job titles at specific company sizes), recruiting can take 6+ weeks on its own.

### Synthetic Research

The same scope — 40 persona interviews on a concept test — runs on a different timeline:

| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Define research questions | 30 minutes |
| Select persona panel | 15 minutes |
| Run study | 20-40 minutes |
| Analysis (auto-generated + manual review) | 1-2 hours |
| **Total** | **2-4 hours** |

There is no recruiting. No scheduling. No no-shows. No transcription backlog. The personas are already built and available.

### What This Means in Practice

A product team planning a sprint can run a synthetic concept test on Monday morning and have directional results by lunch. The equivalent traditional study would deliver results after the next two sprints have already been planned and started.

This speed difference is the primary reason teams adopt synthetic research — not because it's better than talking to real users, but because it fills the gap between "we should research this" and "we already shipped it."

## Cost Comparison

### Traditional Qualitative Research

Costs vary significantly by method, location, and provider. These ranges represent typical US market rates for professional qualitative research:

| Method | Cost Range | What You Get |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| In-depth interviews (10-15 participants) | $12,000-$25,000 | Full transcripts, detailed analysis report, segment insights |
| Focus groups (2-3 groups, 6-8 per group) | $15,000-$35,000 | Moderated discussion, group dynamics, report |
| Online moderated sessions (10-15) | $8,000-$18,000 | Video recordings, transcripts, analysis |
| Diary studies (15-20 participants, 1-2 weeks) | $10,000-$20,000 | Longitudinal data, behavioral patterns |

**What drives the cost:** Participant incentives ($75-$300 per person), recruiter fees, moderator time, facility rental (in-person), transcription, and analyst time for synthesis and reporting.

### Synthetic Research

Synthetic research platforms typically charge subscription pricing:

| Tier | Cost | Volume |
|------|------|--------|
| Individual researcher | $200-$500/month | 10-20 studies/month |
| Team plan | $500-$2,000/month | Unlimited studies, multiple users |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom integrations, API access |

**Cost per study:** Effectively $10-$50 per concept test when amortized across a monthly subscription with regular use. Compared to $800-$2,500 per study for the lowest-cost traditional methods.

### The Math That Matters

Most research teams have a fixed annual budget. The question isn't "which is cheaper?" — it's "how many research questions can I answer with my budget?"

| Annual Budget | Traditional Studies | Synthetic Studies |
|---------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| $50,000 | 2-4 major studies | 100+ concept tests + 2-3 traditional validation studies |
| $100,000 | 5-8 major studies | 200+ concept tests + 4-6 traditional validation studies |
| $150,000 | 8-12 major studies | 300+ concept tests + 6-8 traditional validation studies |

The hybrid approach — using synthetic for screening and traditional for validation — stretches the budget further because you're only spending traditional research dollars on concepts that have already passed a synthetic screen.

## Depth Comparison

This is where the honest assessment gets important.

### Where Traditional Wins on Depth

**Emotional nuance.** A real person pausing, changing their tone, showing visible confusion, or getting genuinely excited about a concept communicates information that text-based synthetic responses cannot replicate. If emotional depth is your primary research output, traditional methods are irreplaceable.

**Unexpected tangents.** Real participants go off-script in revealing ways. A focus group participant mentioning a competitor you didn't ask about, or an interviewee describing a workflow problem you hadn't considered — these serendipitous moments are a strength of traditional qualitative research that synthetic methods can't match.

**Observational data.** Watching someone actually use a product, navigate a prototype, or read your marketing materials produces insights that no simulated persona can generate. Behavioral observation requires real humans.

**Trust and credibility.** When presenting research to stakeholders, "we talked to 15 real customers" carries more weight than "we ran AI personas through a simulation." This matters for buy-in on significant strategic decisions.

### Where Synthetic Wins on Depth

**Breadth of perspectives.** Running 40 personas through a study means you hear from more "voices" than a typical 10-15 person qualitative study. Smaller segments that would be statistically invisible in a traditional study (e.g., 3 out of 40 personas from a specific demographic) become visible enough to flag patterns worth investigating.

**Consistency of analysis.** Every persona response is structured the same way — reasoning, emotional signals, confidence indicators. There's no moderator bias, no dominant participant effect, no social desirability skewing answers.

**Rapid iteration.** You can adjust the stimulus and re-run the same panel in minutes. In traditional research, refining the concept after one round and running a second round adds weeks and thousands of dollars.

**Panel diversity.** Assembling a panel of 40 people spanning 8 different demographics takes weeks and significant budget in traditional research. In synthetic research, it takes 15 minutes and the same subscription cost.

## Reliability Comparison

This is the question researchers care about most, and where we should be most careful with claims.

### What the Validation Data Shows

Based on internal comparisons of synthetic studies with subsequent real-user research on the same topics:

- **Concept preference ranking:** Approximately 78% alignment between synthetic persona preferences and real-user preferences when ranking 3+ concepts
- **Objection identification:** High overlap — the concerns flagged by synthetic panels are generally the same concerns raised by real users
- **Extreme reactions:** Concepts that were strongly positive or strongly negative in synthetic studies maintained that direction in real studies
- **Close calls:** The weakest area. When two concepts are within 5-10% of each other in preference, synthetic research does not reliably predict which will edge ahead with real users

### What This Means

Synthetic research is reliable for screening decisions ("which concepts should we kill?") and directional guidance ("is this resonating with this audience?"). It is not reliable for close-call decisions or final validation.

This is not a limitation to apologize for. Every research method has a reliability profile. Traditional qualitative research with 12 participants isn't statistically representative either — it's also directional, just from a different source.

## Decision Framework

| Your Situation | Recommended Approach |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Early concept screening (5+ ideas) | Synthetic first, traditional for finalists |
| Quick direction before a sprint | Synthetic only |
| Final validation before major launch | Traditional (or synthetic screen → traditional validation) |
| Budget for 2 studies per quarter | Synthetic for everything except the most critical decision |
| Stakeholders require real-user evidence | Traditional (supplement with synthetic for speed) |
| Niche B2B audience hard to recruit | Synthetic for screening, targeted traditional for validation |
| Need ongoing, weekly research | Synthetic (traditional can't sustain that cadence) |

## The Honest Summary

Synthetic research is faster and cheaper. Traditional research is deeper and more credible. Neither is universally better.

The teams getting the most value from synthetic research are the ones who use it to **increase research coverage** — answering questions that would otherwise go unanswered because there wasn't time or budget for a traditional study — rather than as a replacement for real-user research on high-stakes decisions.

If you're currently making most product decisions without any research input because traditional methods are too slow and expensive, synthetic research is a significant upgrade over intuition. If you already have a well-funded research practice, synthetic research extends your reach without replacing your core methodology.
