# From Invisible to Findable: How We Fixed the Technical SEO Blocking a Wealth Management Firm

A financial advisory firm in Henderson, NC had done a lot right.

They had a clear value proposition — fiduciary, real-life financial planning for clients who want someone in their corner, not someone selling them products. They had 23 pages of solid content covering retirement planning, investment management, estate planning, tax planning, and more. Their team had real credentials. Their pricing was competitive.

But when we audited their site, it scored a **4 out of 10 on technical SEO**.

Not because the content was weak. Because the infrastructure underneath it was broken in ways their advisor platform had never told them about.

This is the story of what we found, what we fixed, and what it means for organic visibility going forward.

---

## The Business: Private Wealth Management of NC

Private Wealth Management of NC is an independent Registered Investment Advisor (RIA) based in Henderson, NC. They serve individual and family clients with comprehensive financial planning — retirement, investment, estate, and tax — under a fiduciary standard.

Their differentiator is personal accountability. Not a call center. Not algorithmic portfolios. A real advisor who knows their clients' lives and goals.

That kind of firm lives or dies on trust. And in 2026, trust starts with Google.

## The Audit: 4/10

When Sparkwave AI ran a full technical audit across all 23 pages of the site, five critical issues emerged.

### Issue 1: Zero Schema Markup Sitewide

Not a single structured data block existed anywhere on the site. No `LocalBusiness`, no `FinancialService`, no `Person` markup on team pages, no `Article` markup on blog posts.

This matters because schema is how Google understands what you are — not just what you say. Without it:

- The firm can't appear in Google's local knowledge panel
- Team member profiles can't show up as rich results
- Blog posts won't be attributed to named authors in search
- Services can't be surfaced in structured search features

For a wealth management firm where advisors are the product, being invisible in author search results is a compounding problem.

**The fix:** Add `LocalBusiness` + `FinancialService` schema to the homepage. Add `Person` + `worksFor` to team pages. Add `Article` with author attribution to blog posts. Add `Service` schema to each service page.

**Estimated impact:** Eligibility for local knowledge panels, rich results, and professional profile features on Google.

### Issue 2: No Open Graph Tags on Any Page

Every page was missing `og:title`, `og:description`, and `og:image`.

This sounds like a minor detail. It is not. Financial advisors share content on LinkedIn constantly — market commentary, retirement planning tips, estate planning guides. When a page from this site gets shared on LinkedIn, it renders as a bare URL with no headline, no description, and no image. It looks unprofessional before anyone clicks.

**The fix:** Add standard Open Graph tags to every page template. This is a one-time fix in the CMS theme that applies globally.

**Estimated impact:** Immediate improvement in how shared links appear on LinkedIn, Facebook, and any platform that reads OG tags.

### Issue 3: All Pages Fail Google's Speed Threshold

Every page on the site had a Time to First Byte (TTFB) above Google's 0.8 second threshold:

| Page | TTFB | Status |
|------|------|--------|
| Homepage | 0.98s | ❌ Fails |
| /services | 1.50s | ❌ Fails |
| /blog | 1.22s | ❌ Fails |
| /contact | 0.83s | ❌ Fails |

The root cause: The managed Drupal platform marks every page `UNCACHEABLE` — meaning every request hits the application server fresh. There is no edge caching, no CDN serving cached pages. The `/services` page at 1.5 seconds is particularly problematic, since that's the most commercially valuable page on the site.

**The fix:** Contact the platform vendor (Advisor Websites) and request CDN/edge caching to be enabled. This is a platform configuration, not a code change. If the platform can't enable caching, escalate to their support team.

**Estimated impact:** Potential 40-60% TTFB reduction if caching is enabled. Page speed is a confirmed Google ranking factor.

### Issue 4: H2 Tags Contain Raw JavaScript

On most interior pages — services, who-we-serve, contact, team, FAQ — the H2 heading tags contained raw JavaScript template strings:

```
"0' + (index + 1) + '"
"' + jQuery(this).html() + '"
```

This is a Drupal/jQuery rendering bug. Dynamic heading numbering code was being injected as literal heading content. The practical effect: Google was crawling JavaScript code where subheadings should be. The content hierarchy on interior pages was invisible to search crawlers.

**The fix:** This is a platform bug. Report it to Advisor Websites with specific pages and heading examples. It may require a platform update or theme patch.

**Estimated impact:** Once fixed, interior page content hierarchy becomes crawlable. Potential ranking improvement on interior service and team pages.

### Issue 5: 13 Months of Blog Dormancy

The blog had been inactive for over a year.

This matters for two reasons. First, Google uses content freshness as a signal for some query types, especially financial and advisory queries where recency indicates active expertise. Second, dormant blogs send a signal that the business may not be actively operating or engaged.

**The fix:** Restart the blog with one post per month minimum. Topics should align with what prospects are searching — retirement planning timelines, tax-loss harvesting basics, what fiduciary really means, how to evaluate a financial advisor.

**Estimated impact:** Gradual improvement in crawl frequency and content freshness signals. First post should target a long-tail keyword with clear search intent.

## The Broader Lesson

Most professional services firms have the same problem: strong expertise, weak technical infrastructure.

The advisor platform handles hosting. The advisor assumes "handled" means "optimized." It rarely does.

**The gap:** Advisor platforms are built to make compliance and content management easy. They are not built to win the technical SEO game. The schema your platform doesn't add, the caching it doesn't enable, the OG tags it doesn't include — those are all gaps that compound over time.

An independent technical audit — not done by your platform vendor — is the only way to know what's actually blocking you.
