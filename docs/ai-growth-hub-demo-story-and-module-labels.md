# AI Growth Hub Demo Story + Vertical Module Labels

Issue: SPA-5889
Date: 2026-05-13
Owner: Strategist
Use: buyer-facing demo narrative and label source for the client/demo shell.

## Strategy in one line

Sparkwave installs an AI Growth Hub that helps high-value service businesses stop losing leads, respond faster, follow up consistently, and see what needs attention.

Do not lead with software, technical setup, or internal tools. Lead with the business problem: good leads are getting missed because response and follow-up are too slow or inconsistent.

## Buyer-facing demo story

### Opening

Most businesses do not need more dashboards. They need fewer missed opportunities.

When a lead asks about an appointment, estimate, treatment, product, or consultation, the first few minutes matter. If the response is slow, if the follow-up is inconsistent, or if no one can see which leads are getting stale, revenue quietly leaks out of the business.

Sparkwave installs a business-specific AI Growth Hub so the owner and team can see new leads, respond faster, follow up, and know what needs attention each day.

### What the demo should show

1. Lead activity is visible.
   The owner can see new leads, where each lead came from, who has been contacted, who booked, and who is getting stale.

2. Response is faster.
   The demo shows the first reply and the follow-up path that starts as soon as someone inquires.

3. Follow-up is not left to memory.
   The system shows which leads need the next touch, which old leads can be revived, and which prospects need a human handoff.

4. The Business Brain points out what matters.
   A daily report highlights leads needing attention, common questions, follow-up gaps, content ideas, and recommended next actions.

5. The Growth Agent helps the team ask plain-English questions.
   Example questions: Which leads need follow-up today? Where are we losing people? What should we post about this week? What should we improve next?

6. Content and SEO are the expansion layer.
   The demo should show a small preview of content ideas based on real inquiries, but not make content the main pitch before the lead leak is understood.

### Close

The promise is simple: fewer missed leads, faster response, better follow-up, and a clearer view of what needs attention.

## Recommended first demo sequence

Default: build/sell med spa first, dental second, home services third, Hood River Organic-style farm as a warm, specific local/storytelling demo.

Reason:

- Med spa and dental have high lead value, easy-to-understand consult booking, strong urgency around response time, and clear content expansion.
- Home services is broad and practical, especially for missed calls and estimates.
- Farm is emotionally specific and useful for Hood River Organic-style storytelling, but it should not replace the higher-ticket service-business wedge unless the buyer/context requires it.

## Claims boundaries

Safe demo language:

- Helps you respond faster.
- Helps your team see which leads need follow-up.
- Helps organize lead and follow-up activity.
- Shows daily recommendations about what needs attention.
- Supports content ideas based on customer questions.

Avoid unless verified with client data:

- We will increase revenue by a specific percentage.
- We guarantee booked appointments.
- We replace your front desk or sales team.
- We already know your exact missed revenue.
- We can use production customer data without approval.
- We can send messages, publish content, or change tracking without approval.

## Channel and CTA guidance

Best channel for the demo: sales call, Loom walkthrough, or in-person owner demo.

Primary CTA: Let us map where your leads are coming from and where follow-up is getting missed.

Secondary CTA: Pick one lead source and we will show the follow-up path we would install first.

Approval needed before moving beyond static/demo work:

- permission to connect live lead sources;
- permission to inspect CRM/form/call data;
- approval of outbound/follow-up message language;
- approval before any live sending, publishing, ad spend, tracking changes, customer data import, or CRM migration.

## Risks

1. Too broad: if every feature is shown equally, the buyer may miss the main reason to care. Keep the first story on missed leads.
2. Too technical: avoid technical setup terms and internal product language.
3. Overclaiming: use demo-safe language until lead sources, booking data, and response baselines are verified.
4. Wrong vertical skin: labels should match the buyer's actual workflow; do not force med spa labels onto dental/home/farm demos.
5. Content distraction: content and SEO are expansion layers, not the opening wedge.
