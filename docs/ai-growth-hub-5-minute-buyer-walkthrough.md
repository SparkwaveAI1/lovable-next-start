# AI Growth Hub 5-Minute Buyer Walkthrough

Issue: SPA-5908
Owner: Strategist
Date: 2026-05-13
Use: exact first-call demo talk track for the implemented `/growth-hub` and Lead Dashboard surfaces.

## Demo objective

Make the buyer understand one thing fast: Sparkwave helps them stop losing leads by giving them a simple AI Growth Hub for lead visibility, speed-to-lead, follow-up, Business Brain insights, and a Growth Agent they can ask questions.

Do not present this as generic software, an internal dashboard, or an agent architecture demo. Lead with missed leads and response/follow-up gaps.

## Route order to open

1. `/growth-hub` — opening frame and product map.
2. `/crm` — Lead Dashboard / lead leakage front door.
3. `/fight-flow` — speed-to-lead and follow-up path.
4. `/analytics` — Business Brain daily report / what needs attention.
5. `/mission-control` — Ask Your Growth Agent.
6. Optional only if time or buyer asks: `/content-strategy` — content/SEO expansion preview.

## 5-minute talk track

### 0:00-0:30 — Open `/growth-hub`

Say:
"Most businesses do not need another dashboard. They need fewer missed opportunities. When a good lead comes in, the first response, the next follow-up, and the handoff to a person all have to happen reliably. The AI Growth Hub is the system we install so your team can see new leads, respond faster, follow up consistently, and know what needs attention each day."

Point to:
- Headline: "We help you stop losing leads."
- The five demo-critical surfaces.

Do not say:
- "agentic architecture"
- "learning layer"
- "semantic monitoring"
- "autonomous CRM migration"
- any revenue/CAC/CPA guarantee

Transition:
"Let me start where the money usually leaks first: the leads themselves."

### 0:30-1:30 — Open `/crm` Lead Dashboard

Say:
"This is the Lead Dashboard. The point is simple: every lead should have a status, a source, recent activity, and a next action. On a live install, this becomes the place the owner or team checks to see who is new, who was contacted, who booked, and who is starting to go stale."

Point to:
- Page title: "Lead Dashboard"
- Total contacts / lead count / booked or needs-follow-up indicators if visible.
- Contact list, source, status, pipeline stage, last activity.
- Recent communication evidence if visible.

Say:
"We are not asking the team to remember every follow-up in their head. We want the system to make the next action obvious: this person needs a reply, this person needs a second touch, this person is ready for a human handoff, and this person may be going cold."

CTA line:
"The first question this screen answers is: which leads need attention today?"

Claims boundary:
"Before we make live performance claims, we verify the actual lead sources, response path, and booking/status data. This demo is showing the workflow, not claiming a guaranteed outcome."

Transition:
"Once the lead is visible, the next problem is speed. What happens right after someone inquires?"

### 1:30-2:20 — Open `/fight-flow` Speed-to-Lead

Say:
"This is the speed-to-lead layer. When a form fill, missed call, message, or inquiry comes in, this is where we map the first response, the follow-up sequence, and the human handoff point. The goal is not to replace the team. The goal is to make sure the first touch and next touch are not delayed or forgotten."

Point to:
- Any flow, automation, task, message queue, or FightFlow dashboard elements available.
- First response path.
- Follow-up / handoff / review points.

Say:
"For a med spa, that might be a consultation inquiry. For dental, it might be an implant or Invisalign consult. For home services, it might be an estimate request or missed call. The labels change by business, but the core problem is the same: respond fast, follow up clearly, and know when a human needs to step in."

CTA line:
"The question this screen answers is: what happens the moment someone raises their hand?"

Claims boundary:
"We do not turn on live sending or customer follow-up until the owner approves the message language and the connector path has been tested."

Transition:
"Now we have leads visible and response mapped. The next layer is: what is the system noticing across the business?"

### 2:20-3:20 — Open `/analytics` Business Brain

Say:
"This is the Business Brain. Think of it like an analyst quietly reviewing what happened today and telling you what matters. It watches activity across leads, follow-up, content, campaigns, and automations, then turns that into a plain-English report: what changed, what is slipping, and what should happen next."

Point to:
- Any daily report, learning, analytics, insights, or recommendations shown.
- Risks / opportunities / recommended next actions.
- Evidence or confidence boundaries if present.

Say:
"The Business Brain is not permission to do risky things automatically. It recommends and explains. Actions like live outreach, publishing, ad spend, tracking changes, or sensitive customer-data work still need approval."

Example buyer-safe lines:
- "These leads need a follow-up today."
- "These questions are showing up repeatedly."
- "This follow-up path looks stale."
- "This topic could become content because customers are already asking about it."

CTA line:
"The question this screen answers is: what needs attention that the team might otherwise miss?"

Transition:
"And if the owner wants to ask a plain-English question instead of reading a report, that is where the Growth Agent comes in."

### 3:20-4:10 — Open `/mission-control` Growth Agent

Say:
"This is the Growth Agent surface. The idea is that each business gets a dedicated assistant that understands the offer, customers, lead flow, campaigns, and priorities. The buyer does not need to know the internal system. They can ask practical questions in plain English."

Suggested questions to say aloud:
- "Which leads need follow-up today?"
- "Where are we losing people?"
- "What did customers ask about this week?"
- "What should we post about this week?"
- "What should we improve next?"

Say:
"The Growth Agent helps the owner and team see what to do next. It can draft or recommend, but approvals still control what gets sent, published, changed, or connected."

CTA line:
"The question this screen answers is: what should I do next?"

Transition:
"The main wedge is lead response and follow-up. Once that is credible, we can expand the same system into content and SEO."

### 4:10-4:40 — Optional open `/content-strategy`

Say only if useful:
"This is the expansion layer. Once we understand what prospects are asking and where leads are getting stuck, those questions can become content ideas, local SEO topics, nurture emails, FAQs, and social posts. But we do not make content the main pitch before the lead leak is understood."

Point to:
- Strategy queue / content ideas / approval surface if visible.

Claims boundary:
"Drafting and planning can happen now. Publishing requires approval."

### 4:40-5:00 — Close

Say:
"The promise is simple: fewer missed leads, faster response, better follow-up, and a clearer view of what needs attention. The next step is not a big migration. The next step is to pick one lead source, map the response and follow-up path, and show what the first installed version would look like for your business."

Primary CTA:
"Let us map where your leads are coming from and where follow-up is getting missed."

Secondary CTA:
"Pick one lead source and we will show the follow-up path we would install first."

## If the buyer asks "what is this exactly?"

Say:
"It is an AI Growth Hub installed around your business. It connects lead visibility, response and follow-up workflows, daily Business Brain insights, and a Growth Agent your team can ask questions. We start with lead leakage because that is usually the easiest business problem to understand and validate."

## If the buyer asks "does it automatically message customers?"

Say:
"Only after approval. In a demo or setup phase we can map and draft the flow, but live sending requires approved message language, verified connectors, and a tested handoff path."

## If the buyer asks "will this increase revenue?"

Say:
"The system is designed to reduce missed opportunities by improving visibility, response, and follow-up. We do not promise a specific revenue number without your actual lead volume, response baseline, booking data, and follow-up history."

## Strategist recommendation

Target: high-value service businesses where missed leads are expensive: med spa first, dental second, home services third.

Problem: response is slow, follow-up is inconsistent, and owners cannot easily see stale opportunities.

Offer/promise: install an AI Growth Hub that helps the business respond faster, follow up consistently, and see what needs attention.

Proof needed: demo-safe sample data now; verified lead source, response-time baseline, booking/status source, approved message language, and privacy/compliance checks before live claims.

CTA: "Let us map where your leads are coming from and where follow-up is getting missed."

Channel: first-call demo, Loom walkthrough, or in-person owner walkthrough.

Risks: over-explaining internal architecture; making unsupported revenue guarantees; implying live messaging/publishing/CRM migration before approval; showing too many app surfaces and losing the lead leakage story.
