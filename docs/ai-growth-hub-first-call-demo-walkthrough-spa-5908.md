# AI Growth Hub First-Call Demo Walkthrough

Issue: SPA-5908
Date: 2026-05-13
Owner: Strategist
Use: exact 5-minute buyer walkthrough for the implemented `/growth-hub` and Lead Dashboard surfaces.

## Demo principle

Do not demo the whole product. Demo the buyer problem:

"You are losing leads when response and follow-up are slow or inconsistent. Sparkwave installs an AI Growth Hub so your team can see new leads, respond faster, follow up, and know what needs attention."

Keep the language simple:
- stop losing leads
- speed-to-lead
- follow-up
- Business Brain
- Growth Agent
- what needs attention

Do not say:
- agentic architecture
- learning layer
- context ingestion
- semantic monitoring
- guaranteed revenue lift
- guaranteed booked appointments
- autonomous CRM migration
- live sends are active unless verified and approved

## Route order for a 5-minute buyer walkthrough

1. `/growth-hub` — set the story and show the five demo surfaces.
2. `/crm` — show the Lead Dashboard as the front door.
3. `/fight-flow` — show speed-to-lead and first-response evidence.
4. `/analytics` — show the Business Brain / Learner report surface.
5. `/mission-control` — frame the Growth Agent / ask-your-agent surface.
6. `/content-strategy` — optional 20-second expansion preview only if time remains.

Primary CTA at the end:

"Let us map where your leads are coming from and where follow-up is getting missed."

Secondary CTA:

"Pick one lead source and we will show the follow-up path we would install first."

## Exact 5-minute talk track

### 0:00-0:40 — Open `/growth-hub`

What to click/open:
- Open `/growth-hub`.
- Point to the headline: "We help you stop losing leads."
- Point to the demo-critical surfaces cards.

Say:

"Most businesses do not need another dashboard. They need fewer missed opportunities. When someone fills out a form, calls, texts, or asks about a consultation or estimate, the first few minutes matter. If the response is slow or the follow-up is inconsistent, that lead can go cold before anyone notices.

This is the AI Growth Hub. The simple promise is: see every lead, respond faster, follow up consistently, and know what needs attention each day."

Transition:

"I am going to show this in the order an owner actually cares about it: first the leads, then the first response, then the Business Brain, then the Growth Agent."

### 0:40-1:45 — Open `/crm` Lead Dashboard

What to click/open:
- Click "Open lead dashboard" or open `/crm`.
- Point to the page title: "Lead Dashboard."
- Point to the cards for Contacts, Leads, Follow-up, Phone, and Booked.
- Point to the contact/source/status/last activity table.
- Point to "Recent communications" if visible.

Say:

"This is the front door. The first question is not 'what software are you using?' It is 'which leads need attention right now?'

Here we can see lead records, their source, current status, last activity, and whether there are booking or follow-up signals. This helps the team stop managing leads from memory. If a lead is new, stale, not booked, or missing follow-up, it should be visible here.

For a med spa, this might be consultation leads. For a dental practice, new patient or implant consult inquiries. For a home services company, estimate requests and open quotes. The labels can change by business, but the operating problem is the same: every lead needs a next action."

Safe claim boundary:

"This is demo-safe visibility. Before we make production claims, we verify the actual lead source, response baseline, booking/status source, and data handling rules."

Transition:

"Once leads are visible, the next question is: what happens immediately after someone inquires?"

### 1:45-2:40 — Open `/fight-flow` Speed-to-Lead

What to click/open:
- Open `/fight-flow`.
- Point to "Lead Response / Speed-to-Lead."
- Point to captured lead, first response, speed-to-lead, follow-up / booking evidence, and quality review sections.

Say:

"This is the speed-to-lead view. The goal is to show the first response path: lead captured, first reply, follow-up or booking signal, and whether a human needs to review anything.

This is where the revenue leak gets concrete. If a lead came in and nobody answered, we should see that. If the first response went out, we should see evidence. If the lead needs a human handoff, we should see that too.

The buyer does not need to understand the automation plumbing. They need to know: did we answer quickly, what happened next, and who needs to step in?"

Safe claim boundary:

"We should say 'helps respond faster' and 'shows response evidence.' Do not say it guarantees booked appointments or replaces the front desk. Any live message language needs approval before activation."

Transition:

"Now that the system can see activity, the next value layer is what it learns from that activity."

### 2:40-3:35 — Open `/analytics` Business Brain

What to click/open:
- Open `/analytics`.
- Point to evidence/confidence/review language where visible.
- Frame the page as the Business Brain daily report surface.

Say:

"This is the Business Brain. Think of it like an analyst quietly reviewing the business every day and telling you what needs attention.

The Business Brain is not just another chatbot. It watches activity: leads, follow-up, messages, bookings, content opportunities, and operational signals. Then it turns that into a plain-English report: what changed, what might be slipping, what customers are asking about, and what to do next.

In a real client install, this is where we would want questions like: Which leads need follow-up today? Where are we losing people? What topics keep coming up? Which follow-up assumptions should we check?"

Safe claim boundary:

"Keep the language evidence-bound: the report can recommend next actions based on available signals. It should not pretend to know exact missed revenue or make unsupported performance claims."

Transition:

"The final surface is how the team asks the system plain-English questions."

### 3:35-4:25 — Open `/mission-control` Growth Agent surface

What to click/open:
- Open `/mission-control`.
- Point to agent/task/activity surfaces as the current internal implementation of an agent command surface.
- Frame it as the future/client-facing "Ask Your Growth Agent" module, not as internal ops complexity.

Say:

"This is the idea behind the Growth Agent. For a client, we would simplify this into 'Ask Your Growth Agent.' The team should be able to ask plain questions like:

- Which leads need follow-up today?
- Which opportunities are going stale?
- What did customers ask about this week?
- What should we post about this week?
- What should we improve next?

The important part is that the agent is specific to the business. It understands the offers, lead sources, follow-up paths, content priorities, and what the Business Brain is noticing."

Safe claim boundary:

"Do not imply the agent can take live customer-facing action without approval. The first promise is visibility, draft support, recommendations, and approved handoffs."

Transition:

"There is also an expansion layer, but I would not lead the sales call with it."

### 4:25-4:45 — Optional `/content-strategy` expansion preview

What to click/open:
- Open `/content-strategy` only if there is time.
- Point to content/review/approval language.

Say:

"Once the lead response system is credible, the same hub can support content and SEO. The useful angle is not random content. It is content based on what customers are already asking: treatment questions, service-area questions, estimate objections, financing questions, seasonal product questions.

This stays approval-gated. Drafts, campaigns, and publishing do not go live without approval."

Transition:

"So the first install is not 'let us automate everything.' It is 'let us fix the lead leak first.'"

### 4:45-5:00 — Close and CTA

Say:

"The promise is simple: fewer missed leads, faster response, better follow-up, and a clearer view of what needs attention.

The best next step is not to connect everything at once. It is to map your lead sources and find where follow-up is getting missed. Then we pick one lead source and show the first response and follow-up path we would install first."

Ask:

"Which lead source should we map first: forms, calls, texts, ads, or your CRM?"

## Strategy handoff

Target:
High-value service businesses where missed leads are expensive: med spas, dental/ortho/implants, and high-ticket home services.

Problem:
Slow response, inconsistent follow-up, stale opportunities, and unclear ownership cause leads to slip away.

Offer / promise:
Install an AI Growth Hub that helps the business respond faster, follow up consistently, and see what needs attention.

Proof needed:
Demo-safe sample/visible app data now. Before production claims or activation: verified lead sources, response baseline, booking/status source, approved follow-up language, privacy/compliance review where relevant, and explicit approval before live sends or customer data import.

CTA:
Primary: "Let us map where your leads are coming from and where follow-up is getting missed."
Secondary: "Pick one lead source and we will show the follow-up path we would install first."

Channel:
Sales call, Loom walkthrough, or in-person owner demo. Can later become Jerry/Larry sales copy after approval.

Risks:
- Too broad: showing every product feature buries the lead-leak story.
- Too technical: internal SW app and Hermes language may confuse buyers.
- Overclaiming: no guaranteed booked appointments, revenue lift, CPA/CAC, or live automation claims without proof and approval.
- Compliance: dental/medical demos require privacy-safe data and approved treatment/patient language.
- Premature activation: no live sends, publishing, ad spend, CRM migration, tracking changes, or customer data mutation without explicit approval.
